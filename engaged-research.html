Engaged Research Approach

Engaged research involves collaborative knowledge creation with community partners, stakeholders, and end-users. This approach to engaged research is characterized by:

  • Co-Creation: Working alongside community partners to define research questions, methodologies, and outcomes.
  • Reciprocity: Ensuring mutual benefit for both academic and community partners.
  • Real-World Impact: Focusing on research that addresses practical challenges and leads to tangible solutions.
  • Knowledge Exchange: Facilitating two-way learning between academic expertise and community wisdom.

This approach enables research that is not only academically rigorous but also socially relevant and impactful.

Community Partnerships & Projects

Mayo Greenhouse Partners

Partner: Mayo County Council

Duration: 2022-present

Description: Collaborative research project on data literacy and sustainable practices, connecting academic expertise with local agricultural initiatives to enhance data-driven decision-making and environmental sustainability.

Outcomes: Improved data literacy among community partners, enhanced sustainable practices, and development of scalable models for rural innovation.

Global Play2Learn Academy

Partner: International education stakeholders in Ireland, Botswana, and Ukraine

Duration: 2021-present

Description: A research initiative examining how play-based mathematics teaching approaches can widen STEM access across international contexts, incorporating learning analytics to measure engagement and outcomes.

Outcomes: Implementation of play-based methodologies in educational settings across multiple countries, demonstrating the global impact of this research-informed approach to mathematics education.

REAL Assessment Wizard

Partner: QQI (Quality and Qualifications Ireland)

Duration: 2020-2022

Description: Development of an assessment toolkit supporting reflective learning practices, equity-focused assessment design, authentic task development, and learning-centered assessment protocols.

Outcomes: Implementation across multiple departments at ATU, with external examiners describing it as "a model for other disciplines."

"The Play2Learn methodology has transformed how our teachers approach mathematics education, making it more accessible and engaging for students across diverse backgrounds. The learning analytics component has been invaluable in tracking progress and adapting our approaches."

Industry Collaborations

Bridging academic research with industry needs creates opportunities for innovation and practical application. Current industry collaborations include:

RCSI and NIBRT Biopharma Initiative

Partner: Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland (RCSI) and National Institute for Bioprocessing Research and Training (NIBRT)

Focus Area: Level 7 Online Biopharma Programmes

Activities: Collaborative development of educational frameworks that bridge academic research with industry needs in the biopharmaceutical sector, designing accessible online learning pathways.

Innovations: Creation of specialized online learning modules that incorporate industry-standard practices and responsive assessment models.

Computational Mathematics Programme

Partner: Atlantic Technological University and Industry Advisory Board

Focus Area: Ireland's first BSc in Computational Mathematics

Activities: Leading the development of innovative curriculum that integrates AI, quantum computing, and financial mathematics with input from industry partners.

Innovations: Creation of a pioneering programme designed to address industry needs in computational fields while maintaining academic rigor and student-centered learning approaches.

These collaborations exemplify a commitment to research that addresses real-world challenges and contributes to innovation in mathematics and learning technology.
