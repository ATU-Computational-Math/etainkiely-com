Global Play2Learn

Our PLAY framework cultivates collaboration, knowledge transfer, real-world application, and active engagement within global learning communities. By making learning fun and engaging through gamification, we unlock creativity and critical thinking skills while driving sustainability through education.

Explore Initiatives View Impact

Key Initiatives

🌍

Global Agri-PLAY with BUAN

A collaborative project with Botswana University of Agriculture and Natural Resources (BUAN) that blends agricultural science with mathematical modeling through game-based learning approaches.

Students engage with real-world agricultural challenges using mathematical tools, developing sustainable solutions that address local farming challenges.

Fraction Action & Superpower Learning

Fractions become superpowers in this high-energy learning initiative. Students develop mathematical fluency while engaging in gamified challenges that make abstract concepts tangible and fun.

This initiative transforms the traditionally challenging topic of fractions into an exciting adventure through storytelling and character-based learning.

🧩

Playground Science & Maths in Motion

Transforming ordinary playgrounds into living mathematics laboratories where children discover physical principles through play.

This initiative connects kinesthetic learning with mathematical concepts, helping students experience abstract ideas through physical movement and play.

🎮

ATU Metaverse Minecraft Project

Utilizing the popular Minecraft platform to create immersive mathematical learning environments where students solve problems in virtual spaces.

This project bridges digital literacy with mathematical problem-solving, creating engaging learning experiences that resonate with digital natives.

💻

Code Meets Context: STEM Labs

A series of coding-based workshops that integrate mathematical principles with computer science, inspired by natural patterns and real-world applications.

These workshops provide free resources like code notebooks inspired by natural patterns and Leaving Cert Computer Science materials.

Impact Metrics

Global Partnerships

Play2Learn has established meaningful educational partnerships across multiple countries including:

  • Ukraine: Supporting mathematical education in conflict-affected areas
  • Botswana: Agricultural mathematics education and sustainability
  • Malaysia: Computational thinking and mathematics integration
  • Nigeria: Game-based learning for large classroom environments
  • Ireland: Cross-cultural mathematics education partnerships

These partnerships have created a global learning community that shares resources, methodologies, and outcomes, enhancing mathematical education worldwide.

🇺🇦 Ukraine Mathematical education in conflict-affected areas

Digital learning resources supporting mathematics education in conflict zones, with a focus on accessible offline learning.

Outcome: 5 schools equipped with Play2Learn curriculum materials

Contact: Prof. Mykola Zhurovskyi, Kyiv Polytechnic Institute

🇧🇼 Botswana Agricultural mathematics education

Collaborative initiative with BUAN integrating mathematical modeling with sustainable farming practices through gamification.

Outcome: 12 agricultural mathematics modules developed and implemented

Contact: Dr. Thuto Baitseng, BUAN

🇲🇾 Malaysia Computational thinking integration

Partnership focusing on integrating computational thinking into mathematics curriculum through culturally-relevant problem solving.

Outcome: 4 teacher training workshops conducted with 85 participants

Contact: Dr. Mei Ling Tan, Universiti Sains Malaysia

🇳🇬 Nigeria Game-based learning for large classrooms

Developing scalable game-based learning approaches for mathematics education in large classroom environments.

Outcome: 8 schools implementing Play2Learn methods, reaching 1,200+ students

Contact: Dr. Oluwaseun Adeleke, University of Lagos

🇮🇪 Ireland Cross-cultural education partnerships

ATU-based initiatives coordinating global Play2Learn activities and developing new teaching methodologies.

Outcome: Establishment of Global Play2Learn Academy, coordinating all international initiatives

Contact: Dr. Etain Kiely, Atlantic Technological University
