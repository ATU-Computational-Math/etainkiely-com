Teaching Philosophy

"In true dialogue, both sides are willing to change." — Thích Nhất Hạnh

This quote encapsulates my approach to education. I believe that teaching is not merely about transferring knowledge but creating spaces where genuine dialogue can flourish. My teaching philosophy centers on:

  • Inclusive Learning Design: Creating accessible, engaging mathematics experiences that meet diverse learner needs and build confidence in STEM disciplines.
  • Analytics-Driven Pedagogy: Using learning analytics to identify intervention points, personalize learning pathways, and continuously improve teaching approaches.
  • Mastery-Based Assessment: Implementing formative assessment models that emphasize growth, reflection, and mastery rather than high-stakes testing alone.

In practice, this means developing learning environments where students can explore mathematical concepts through curiosity-led approaches, receive timely and constructive feedback, and develop a growth mindset toward mathematics and STEM subjects.

Teaching Innovation & Impact

Moodle Maths Sandbox

A comprehensive digital mathematics learning environment mapped to National Framework Qualification (NFQ) levels. This innovative platform provides:

  • Adaptive learning pathways calibrated to student ability
  • Over 20,000 annual quiz attempts with automated feedback
  • Embedded analytics for early intervention
  • Gamified elements to enhance motivation and engagement

Impact: Increased class attendance from <40% to ~80% and significantly improved student satisfaction and performance.

REAL Assessment Wizard

A QQI-funded assessment toolkit that supports:

  • Reflective learning practices through structured feedback
  • Equity-focused assessment design
  • Authentic task development aligned with real-world applications
  • Learning-centered assessment protocols that emphasize growth

Impact: Implemented across multiple departments at ATU, with external examiners describing it as "a model for other disciplines."

NanoLab Platform

A virtual postgraduate STEM learning environment that facilitates:

  • Remote access to advanced computational tools
  • Collaborative research spaces for distributed teams
  • Integration of industry-standard software and practices
  • Simulated laboratory experiences for distance learners
