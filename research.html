Research Overview

This research examines the intersection of mathematics education, learning analytics, and artificial intelligence, with a particular focus on developing inclusive pedagogical approaches that enhance student success. The work employs mixed-method research techniques incorporating data analytics, qualitative assessment, and design-based research to investigate how technology can support deeper learning experiences.

This work contributes to our understanding of how data-informed teaching practices can transform educational outcomes, with direct applications in mathematics education, STEM disciplines, and broader educational policy development.

Research Focus Areas

Learning Analytics & Educational Data Mining

Using data to understand and optimize student learning pathways, with particular emphasis on identifying early intervention points and personalized learning opportunities. This research integrates natural language processing tools for self-regulated learning and automated feedback systems.

AI-Enhanced Mathematics Pedagogy

Developing and evaluating innovative approaches to mathematics teaching that leverage artificial intelligence to provide personalized learning experiences. This includes research on mastery-based assessment models and ethical implementation of AI in educational contexts.

Digital Transformation in Education

Investigating how digital tools and platforms can enhance educational practices and outcomes, particularly in higher education STEM disciplines. Research examines the design, implementation, and evaluation of digital learning environments.

Educational Equity & Inclusion

Exploring how technology and data-informed approaches can address equity gaps in mathematics and STEM education, with a focus on increasing participation and success among underrepresented groups.

NanoLab Virtual Postgraduate STEM Platform

Status: Active (2022-present)

Funding: National Forum for the Enhancement of Teaching and Learning

Description: Research and development of virtual environments for postgraduate STEM education, focused on creating accessible, high-quality research training experiences that bridge theory and practice across institutions.

Global Play2Learn Academy

Status: Expanding (2021-present)

Partners: International education stakeholders in Ireland, Botswana, Ukraine and beyond

Description: A research initiative examining how play-based mathematics teaching approaches can widen STEM access across international contexts. Incorporates learning analytics to measure engagement and outcomes across diverse cultural settings.
